Daily Lesson List

LessonTitleFocus Question
1.1Why Engineers SketchWhy do engineers sketch before they build or model in CAD?
1.2Line ConventionsHow do different types of lines help engineers communicate clearly?
1.3Object Lines & Hidden LinesHow can a sketch show features that are part of an object but cannot be seen from one view?
1.4Centerlines & SymmetryHow do engineers show the center of a feature or object in a technical sketch?
1.5Isometric SketchingHow can engineers show a 3D object clearly on a flat page?
1.6Isometric Circles, Arcs, and ShadingHow can engineers show curved features and surface depth in an isometric sketch?
1.7Orthographic ProjectionWhy do engineers use multiple 2D views instead of only one 3D sketch?
1.8Top, Front & Right Side ViewsHow do engineers keep multiple views of the same object organized and accurate?
1.9Dimensioning BasicsHow do engineers show the size of an object clearly in a drawing?
1.10Aerospace Bracket SketchHow can engineers use sketches to communicate the shape, features, and size of a part before it is modeled or built?
1.11Rocket Assembly Reverse EngineeringDisassemble the teacher-provided 3D printed rocket assembly and begin documenting its parts, fits, and functions.
1.12Why Documentation MattersConnect the rocket disassembly to the need for clear engineering documentation.
1.13Measurement ToolsUse rulers, tape measures, calipers, and micrometers to measure rocket parts accurately.
1.14TolerancesDetermine acceptable measurement variation for rocket part fit and alignment.
1.15Adhesives & FastenersCompare joining methods for rocket parts and decide which connections should be removable or permanent.
1.16Rocket Assembly SystemsIdentify rocket parts, subassemblies, connection points, and the function of each part.
1.17Documenting the Rocket AssemblyCreate labeled assembly documentation that explains the rocket parts, connections, materials, and purpose.
1.18Rocket Exploded ViewCreate an exploded view that separates rocket parts while showing how they align and fit together.
1.19Rocket Bill of MaterialsCreate a Bill of Materials that lists all rocket parts, quantities, materials, and notes.
1.20Rocket Assembly NotesWrite clear notes that explain how to reassemble the rocket accurately and safely.
1.21Engineering Change RequestsPropose and document an improvement to the rocket assembly using evidence from the unit.
1.22Final Rocket Assembly Proposal PresentationPresent a proposed rocket assembly improvement supported by the final documentation package.

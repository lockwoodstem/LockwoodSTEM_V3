Course Focus

POE connects engineering science to aerospace systems: power transfer, lightweight structures, automated controls, test data, and motion.

  • Mechanisms and energy systems
  • Materials, statics, and structures
  • Machine control and fluid power
  • Statistics, kinematics, and testing
Scope & Sequence

Course pathway

This page keeps the core PLTW POE structure while reframing each unit around aerospace engineering applications.

Unit 1

Energy & Power in Aerospace Systems

Core topics: mechanisms, simple machines, gears, pulleys, sprockets, work, power, efficiency, energy sources, circuits, solar cells, thermal transfer, insulation, and energy conversion.

Aerospace lens: launch support mechanisms, deployable payload systems, solar power, aircraft and spacecraft energy budgets, thermal protection, and efficient power transfer.

Unit 2

Materials & Aerospace Structures

Core topics: statics, free body diagrams, vectors, moments, centroids, moment of inertia, material properties, manufacturing, recycling, and material testing.

Aerospace lens: lightweight structures, launch towers, trusses, airframe supports, wing spars, material tradeoffs, strength-to-weight ratio, and failure analysis.

Unit 3

Aerospace Control Systems

Core topics: flowcharts, algorithms, open-loop control, closed-loop feedback, digital and analog inputs, sensors, outputs, pneumatics, hydraulics, pressure, flow, and Pascal’s Law.

Aerospace lens: automated payload doors, deployable solar panels, landing gear, launch release systems, sensor feedback, pneumatic actuators, and hydraulic control surfaces.

Unit 4

Flight Data, Statistics & Kinematics

Core topics: probability, histograms, mean, median, mode, standard deviation, variance, displacement, velocity, acceleration, free fall, and projectile motion.

Aerospace lens: flight testing, launch angle analysis, projectile range, glide testing, model rocket motion, experimental variation, and data-driven design decisions.

Aerospace Design Challenges

Possible major projects

Each design challenge is built around the POE concepts while giving students a realistic aerospace problem to solve.

Unit 1

Deployable Aerospace Mechanism

Design, build, and test a mechanical system that redirects force, speed, or motion for a payload door, solar panel, or launch support device.

Unit 2

Lightweight Structure Challenge

Design and test a truss, wing spar, tower, or frame that balances strength, stiffness, mass, material choice, and manufacturability.

Unit 3

Automated Aerospace System

Create a controlled system using sensors, outputs, logic, and/or fluid power to perform a repeatable aerospace-related task.

Unit 4

Flight Test & Data Challenge

Collect and analyze motion data from a launch, vehicle, glider, or projectile system to predict, improve, and communicate performance.

Student Outcomes

What you will be able to do

By the end of this course, you will connect engineering science to the design and testing of aerospace systems.

Analyze systems

Use diagrams, measurements, calculations, and test data to explain how engineering systems transfer energy, carry loads, move, and respond.

Build and test prototypes

Create physical models, test them under controlled conditions, collect evidence, and revise designs based on performance.

Communicate engineering decisions

Use sketches, calculations, graphs, CAD models, presentations, and engineering documentation to justify design choices.
